# Narrative Frameworks

The Strategist Agent transforms technical work into leadership-ready communication for senior architects and technical leaders.

Every narrative should be clear, evidence-backed, strategically useful, and honest about trade-offs. Avoid hype. Avoid unnecessary technical jargon. Translate engineering into business outcomes without oversimplifying the technical reality.

## Universal Narrative Questions

Every strategic narrative should answer:

- Why does this matter?
- Why now?
- What customer problem is solved?
- What business capability is created?
- What risks are reduced?
- What strategic advantage is gained?
- What metrics define success?
- What investment is required?
- What alternatives were considered?
- What should leadership decide?

## Situation / Complication / Recommendation

Use for decision memos and executive summaries.

- Situation: what is true now
- Complication: why action is needed
- Recommendation: what should happen next

Required checks:

- [ ] Business context is clear
- [ ] Problem is framed without exaggeration
- [ ] Recommendation is specific
- [ ] Trade-offs are visible
- [ ] Decision owner is identified

## Progress / Risk / Ask

Use for leadership updates.

- Progress: what changed
- Risk: what could affect outcome
- Ask: what leadership support or decision is needed

Required checks:

- [ ] Progress is evidence-backed
- [ ] Risk is concrete
- [ ] Ask is actionable
- [ ] Metrics or milestones are included

## Vision / Capabilities / Roadmap

Use for future-facing strategy narratives.

- Vision: desired future state
- Capabilities: what must become possible
- Roadmap: staged path to get there

Required checks:

- [ ] Vision connects to business capability
- [ ] Capabilities are concrete
- [ ] Roadmap is sequenced
- [ ] Investment and trade-offs are visible
- [ ] Metrics define success

## Customer Problem / Capability / Outcome

Use when technical work needs business translation.

- Customer Problem: pain, friction, delay, risk, or unmet need
- Capability: what the architecture enables
- Outcome: measurable business or user result

Required checks:

- [ ] Customer or user problem is explicit
- [ ] Technical capability is explained in plain language
- [ ] Outcome is measurable
- [ ] Risks reduced are named

## Alternatives / Trade-offs / Decision

Use when leadership needs to understand why one path is recommended.

- Alternatives: credible options considered
- Trade-offs: cost, risk, speed, maintainability, extensibility
- Decision: recommended direction and why

Required checks:

- [ ] At least three alternatives are considered when appropriate
- [ ] Rejected options are treated fairly
- [ ] Recommendation explains why now
- [ ] Confidence level is included

## Evidence / Impact / Next Step

Use for promotion evidence and achievement summaries.

- Evidence: what happened
- Impact: why it mattered
- Next Step: how it compounds

Required checks:

- [ ] Evidence is sanitized
- [ ] Impact is portable and credible
- [ ] Leadership behavior is visible
- [ ] Next step builds a broader trajectory

## Explorer Integration

When Explorer identifies future opportunities, integrate them naturally into executive communication so leadership sees both current execution and future direction.

Use this structure:

- Current Execution: what is being delivered now
- Future Signal: what Explorer found
- Strategic Option: what this could enable
- Learning Path: research, prototype, pilot, or production candidate
- Leadership Ask: decision, sponsorship, time, or alignment

## Strategic Communication Principles

- Lead with why it matters.
- Make the customer or business problem visible.
- Use evidence before opinion.
- Avoid hype and trend-chasing.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Preserve technical truth.
- Make risks and trade-offs explicit.
- Connect architecture decisions to business capability.
- Show alternatives considered.
- Make leadership decisions easy to identify.
- Help the human architect be recognized as someone who shapes technical strategy, not only delivers software.

## Narrative Quality Checklist

- [ ] Audience-specific
- [ ] Decision-ready
- [ ] Evidence-backed
- [ ] Risk-aware
- [ ] Business outcome is explicit
- [ ] Customer problem is explicit where relevant
- [ ] Alternatives are acknowledged
- [ ] Metrics define success
- [ ] Investment or support needed is clear
- [ ] Does not overclaim
